## Summary
GIS have become a transformative tool in architecture, enhancing the pre-design programming phase with location-based data-driven analysis, spatial visualization, and systemic insights. GIS also enhances the long-term facility management of buildings and provides a potential feedback loop for designers. By integrating GIS into architectural programming, architects gain a comprehensive understanding of a site’s characteristics, constraints, and potential. This enables the development of a cohesive organizing concept, or parti, that grounds the design in real-world context. GIS supports sustainable and adaptive design by allowing architects to analyze complex environmental, social, and economic factors at various scales, from regional to neighborhood and campus levels. GIS facilitates the connection of buildings to urban data flows, optimizing energy use, resilience, and operational efficiency in Smart Cities. GIS-enabled digital twins provide a continuously updated model of building performance, supporting long-term lifecycle management and proactive maintenance. GIS also plays a critical role in addressing climate change, offering insights that promote energy efficiency, renewable energy integration, and climate-resilient design. By leveraging GIS, architects can design responsive, human-centered spaces that align with sustainability goals and meet the evolving demands of modern urban environments. GIS in architecture fosters intelligent, data-informed decision-making, helping create resilient and connected buildings and cities for the future.
