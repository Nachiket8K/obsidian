## Summary
Hyperspectral remote sensing (HRS) is a specialized technique that collects and processes hundreds of contiguous spectral bands across a broad range of electromagnetic wavelengths. Unlike multispectral remote sensing, which records only a few discrete broad bands (e.g., 30 nm) HRS acquires continuous spectra in narrow bandwidths (e.g., 5 nm). This capability provides a richer set of spectral signatures, enabling more precise identification of surface materials, vegetation, and minerals, among other applications. Although HRS applies many of the same physical principles as multispectral remote sensing, it requires specialized instruments, data collection procedures, and analytical methods.
