## Key points
- Simulation Model Building / Routing Entities: In many systems, decisions must be made about the routing of entities from operation to operation. This section discusses typical ways systems make such routing decisions as well as techniques for modeling them.
- The Simulation Engine / Summary: This chapter discusses the basic operations of a simulation engine. While these operations are performed transparently to the modeler, an understanding of them helps clarify how simulation experiments work.
- Job Shops / Introduction: Introduces the chapter’s problem setting and explains why simulation is needed as a practical decision-support tool for this domain.
- Job Shops / Points Made in the Case Study: Highlights the main modelling and decision lessons the chapter wants the reader to carry into later case studies and implementation work.
- Job Shops / The Case Study: Introduces the structured case-study workflow that connects issue framing, model building, root-cause assessment, review, and implementation.
- Job Shops / Define the Issues and Solution Objective: Clarifies the operational problem, the performance measures of interest, and the future-state question the analytic and simulation models must answer.
