# Stocks and flows

## Upward links
- [[The Delft Method for System Dynamics - Source]]

## Summary
Stocks represent accumulations and flows are the rates that change them. Together they form the quantitative backbone of System Dynamics models.

## Downward links
- [[Delft SD Ch 01 - Introduction to System Dynamics]]
- [[Delft SD Ch 03 - Model formulation 1]]
- [[Delft SD Ch 07 - Formulation]]

## Key points
- Stocks provide memory to a system.
- Flows are the only mechanisms through which stocks change.
- Good stock-flow design keeps model meaning, units, and boundaries clear.
