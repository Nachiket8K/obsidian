# Deep uncertainty

## Upward links
- [[DMDU - Source]]
- [[Policy Analysis of Multi-Actor Systems - Source]]

## Summary
In DMDU, deep uncertainty describes situations where actors do not know or cannot agree on the right models, probability distributions, or value judgments. It is the core problem condition that motivates the methods in this book.

## Key points
- Prediction alone is not enough when uncertainty is structural, contested, or irreducible.
- Deep uncertainty often appears in long-term, multi-actor, high-stakes policy problems.
- DMDU methods respond by broadening exploration, building adaptability, and supporting learning.
