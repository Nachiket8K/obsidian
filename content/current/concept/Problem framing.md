# Problem framing

## Upward links
- [[Policy Analysis of Multi-Actor Systems - Source]]

## Summary
Problem framing is the act of presenting and interpreting a problem in a particular way, emphasizing some causes, boundaries, values, and solution directions while downplaying others.

## Downward links
- [[PAMS Ch 02 - Problem Formulation in Complex Environments]]
- [[PAMS Ch 06 - From Synthesis to Plan of Action]]
- [[PAMS Ch 08 - Preparing an Issue Paper]]

## Key points
- Different frames can produce different diagnoses and action proposals.
- Framing is unavoidable in multi-actor settings with contested values.
- Good framing should be explicit enough to be discussed and revised.
