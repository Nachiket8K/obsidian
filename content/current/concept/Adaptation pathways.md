# Adaptation pathways

## Upward links
- [[DMDU - Source]]

## Summary
Adaptation pathways represent sequences of actions that remain viable over time until tipping points are reached. They help planners see timing, switching options, and path dependence more clearly.

## Key points
- Useful when decisions unfold over decades rather than in one step.
- Support flexibility by comparing alternate sequences of actions.
- Make pathway switching and tipping points visible.
