## Summary
GIS-based computational modeling refers to the use of GIS-linked computational models to simulate, forecast, and experiment with geographic systems across time and space. In this vault it is a central bridge note connecting GIS&T with DMDU, System Dynamics, Mesa, and GeoPandas.

## Key points
- GIS can be loosely or tightly coupled with computational models.
- Relevant model families include system dynamics, agent-based models, cellular automata, and hybrid simulation.
- Calibration, sensitivity analysis, validation, and scenario design are integral to trustworthy model use.
