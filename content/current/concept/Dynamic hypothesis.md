# Dynamic hypothesis

## Upward links
- [[The Delft Method for System Dynamics - Source]]

## Summary
A dynamic hypothesis is the proposed explanation of how system structure generates the behaviour of interest. It connects the conceptual model to both formulation and evaluation.

## Downward links
- [[Delft SD Ch 04 - Model formulation 2]]
- [[Delft SD Ch 06 - Problem Formulation and Conceptualisation]]

## Key points
- States why the problem behaviour should arise from the proposed structure.
- Acts as a bridge from conceptualisation to quantitative modelling.
- Supports interpretation by linking behaviour back to model logic.
