# Spatial decision support

## Upward links
- [[GIS&T Body of Knowledge - Source]]

## Summary
Spatial decision support applies decision-support ideas to problems where alternatives, criteria, impacts, or stakeholders are explicitly geographic. In this vault it bridges GIS&T with DMDU by linking geospatial data, models, and scenarios to structured decision processes.

## Key points
- Spatiality changes both what decisions are being made and how evidence is evaluated.
- Spatial decision support often combines GIS, models, visualization, and multicriteria reasoning.
- It is especially useful for semi-structured problems with multiple stakeholders and geospatial trade-offs.
