# Policy process

## Upward links
- [[Policy Analysis of Multi-Actor Systems - Source]]

## Summary
The policy process is the ongoing, iterative, and often contested sequence through which issues are recognized, framed, debated, decided, implemented, and revisited.

## Downward links
- [[PAMS Ch 01 - Introduction]]
- [[PAMS Ch 02 - Problem Formulation in Complex Environments]]

## Key points
- Real policy processes are not fully linear or rational.
- Problem definitions, participants, and opportunities shift over time.
- Analysts support the process rather than controlling it.
