# Levels of uncertainty

## Upward links
- [[DMDU - Source]]

## Summary
The book distinguishes multiple levels of uncertainty, ranging from situations that can be handled with standard probabilistic tools to situations of deep uncertainty and practical ignorance. This spectrum clarifies when conventional risk analysis stops being enough.

## Downward links
- [[DMDU Ch 01 - Introduction]]
- [[DMDU Ch 04 - Dynamic Adaptive Policy Pathways (DAPP)]]

## Key points
- Lower levels fit prediction and probabilistic analysis better.
- Higher levels require robustness, adaptability, and exploratory approaches.
- The distinction helps match method to problem type.
