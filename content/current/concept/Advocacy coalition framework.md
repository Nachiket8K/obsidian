# Advocacy coalition framework

## Upward links
- [[Policy Analysis of Multi-Actor Systems - Source]]

## Summary
The advocacy coalition framework explains policy change through competing coalitions of actors who share beliefs and coordinate over time within a policy domain.

## Downward links
- [[PAMS Ch 02 - Problem Formulation in Complex Environments]]

## Key points
- Policy conflict is structured by coalitions, not just individuals.
- Beliefs, learning, and long-term interaction matter for change.
- The framework is useful for analyzing persistence and contestation in policy subsystems.
