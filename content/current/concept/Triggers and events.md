# Triggers and events

## Upward links
- [[DMDU - Source]]

## Summary
The Planned Adaptation chapter distinguishes triggers and events as mechanisms that call for policy evaluation and possible rule change. These distinctions make adaptive governance more explicit and analysable.

## Downward links
- [[DMDU Ch 13 - A Conceptual Model of Planned Adaptation (PA)]]
- [[DMDU Ch 14 - DMDU into Practice - Adaptive Delta Management in The Netherlands]]

## Key points
- Triggers can be periodic, stochastic, or tacit.
- Events can arise inside the system, outside it, or at its boundary.
- Different trigger-event combinations imply different monitoring and governance needs.
