# Garbage can model

## Upward links
- [[Policy Analysis of Multi-Actor Systems - Source]]

## Summary
The garbage can model treats decision-making in complex organizations as an opportunistic coupling of problems, solutions, participants, and choice opportunities rather than as a clean rational sequence.

## Downward links
- [[PAMS Ch 02 - Problem Formulation in Complex Environments]]

## Key points
- Problems and solutions may circulate independently before being matched.
- Participants and opportunities matter as much as formal problem-solving logic.
- The model helps explain why policy processes often appear messy or nonlinear.
