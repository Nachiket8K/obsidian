# Policy networks

## Upward links
- [[Policy Analysis of Multi-Actor Systems - Source]]

## Summary
Policy networks are the patterned relationships among actors involved in a policy problem, including ties of influence, dependence, information, and coordination.

## Downward links
- [[PAMS Ch 04 - Actor Analysis]]

## Key points
- Network structure shapes who can influence whom.
- Information and resource flows matter alongside formal authority.
- Networks can stabilize cooperation or reproduce conflict.
