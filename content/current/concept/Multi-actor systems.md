# Multi-actor systems

## Upward links
- [[Policy Analysis of Multi-Actor Systems - Source]]

## Summary
Multi-actor systems are problem settings in which many actors with different interests, resources, and perceptions shape outcomes, and no single actor can unilaterally impose a stable solution.

## Downward links
- [[PAMS Ch 01 - Introduction]]
- [[PAMS Ch 04 - Actor Analysis]]

## Key points
- Complexity increases through the number of actors and their interdependencies.
- Divergent values and goals make consensus and implementation difficult.
- These systems require analysis of both substance and governance.
