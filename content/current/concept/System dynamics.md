## Summary
System Dynamics is a modelling and simulation tradition that explains time-dependent behaviour from endogenous structure. It is especially useful when accumulations, delays, and feedback make intuitive reasoning unreliable.

## Key points
- Behaviour is explained from structure rather than from isolated events.
- Stocks, flows, and feedback loops are central building blocks.
- The method is iterative and often used for policy design and learning.
