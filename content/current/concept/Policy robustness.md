# Policy robustness

## Upward links
- [[DMDU - Source]]
- [[EMA Workbench Documentation - Source]]

## Summary
Policy robustness is the idea that a policy should perform acceptably across many plausible futures instead of being optimal for one forecast. It is the common evaluative thread linking nearly all DMDU methods.

## Key points
- Robustness is about acceptable performance across variety, not perfection in one case.
- Different methods operationalize robustness in different ways.
- Robustness often pairs with adaptability when future conditions can shift.
