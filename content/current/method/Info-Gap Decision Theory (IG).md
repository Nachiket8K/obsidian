# Info-Gap Decision Theory (IG)

## Upward links
- [[DMDU - Source]]

## Summary
A robustness-oriented method for decisions made with severe gaps in knowledge. Instead of optimizing expected performance, IG asks how much uncertainty a decision can tolerate while still meeting critical requirements.

## Downward links
- [[DMDU Ch 05 - Info-Gap Decision Theory (IG)]]
- [[DMDU Ch 10 - Info-Gap (IG) - Robust Design of a Mechanical Latch]]

## Core ideas
- Frame uncertainty as a gap between what is known and what must be known.
- Use satisficing thresholds instead of searching only for optima.
- Compare decisions with robustness and opportuneness curves.
- Keep attention on critical outcomes under ignorance.
