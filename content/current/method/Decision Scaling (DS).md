# Decision Scaling (DS)

## Upward links
- [[DMDU - Source]]

## Summary
A climate decision-support approach that starts from what a system can tolerate and only then uses climate information to assess concern. Decision Scaling is useful when forecast disputes would otherwise dominate the decision process.

## Downward links
- [[DMDU Ch 12 - Decision Scaling (DS) - Decision Support for Climate Change]]

## Core ideas
- Start with decision vulnerabilities rather than climate model outputs.
- Run a climate stress test across a broad plausible range.
- Bring climate information in later as a sensitivity factor.
- Support multi-objective public decision processes under uncertainty.
