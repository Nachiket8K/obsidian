# Robust Decision Making (RDM)

## Upward links
- [[DMDU - Source]]

## Summary
A DMDU method that stress-tests candidate strategies across many plausible futures and then uses the resulting evidence to identify robust adaptive strategies. In this book it is presented as both a theory chapter and a pair of applications.

## Downward links
- [[DMDU Ch 02 - Robust Decision Making (RDM)]]
- [[DMDU Ch 07 - Robust Decision Making (RDM) - Application to Water Planning and Climate Policy]]

## Core ideas
- Test strategies against many plausible futures instead of predicting one best future.
- Use vulnerability analysis and scenario discovery to explain where policies fail.
- Prefer robust and adaptive strategies over brittle optima.
- Embed analytics inside deliberation with analysis.
