# System dynamics modelling

## Upward links
- [[The Delft Method for System Dynamics - Source]]

## Summary
A modelling approach that explains behaviour over time from underlying structure, especially stocks, flows, delays, and feedback loops. In this book it is taught as an iterative process that supports learning, communication, and policy design rather than one-shot forecasting.

## Core ideas
- Explain behaviour from endogenous structure rather than isolated events.
- Use accumulations, delays, and feedback as primary modelling building blocks.
- Treat modelling as an iterative cycle from problem formulation to policy testing.
- Use simulation to extend reasoning beyond what mental models can handle.
